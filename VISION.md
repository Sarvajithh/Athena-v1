# VISION.md — Project Athena

## What Athena Is

Athena is a single-user Personal Operating System whose job is to sit between
you and every decision that determines whether you become the person who gets
into Imperial/Oxford for an MSc in Mathematics and lands a high-paying
ML/Quant career — or the person who almost did.

Athena is not a productivity app you check. It is infrastructure you operate
inside of. A calendar tells you what's happening. A to-do list tells you
what's pending. Athena tells you **what matters right now, and why, given
everything else that is true about your life this week** — and it does this
automatically, continuously, without being asked.

## The Core Bet

Your stated bottleneck is not laziness and not procrastination in the
classic sense. It's **allocation**: you have the hours, you do the work, but
the work you do in a given hour is frequently not the highest-leverage work
available to you at that moment. Athena exists to close that gap.

Every feature, every alert, every recommendation Athena produces should be
justifiable by one test:

> "Does this reduce the number of decisions [User] has to make about WHAT to
> work on, and does it bias those decisions toward Future-Him over
> Present-Him?"

If a feature doesn't pass this test, it doesn't belong in Athena, no matter
how useful it looks in isolation.

## The End State (3–5 years out)

- CGPA trajectory sits at or above 8.8, with early-warning course-correction
  happening a full semester before any dip could become structural.
- A DSA/Codeforces practice history dense enough, and a project/research
  portfolio sharp enough, to be genuinely competitive for ML/Quant
  internships — not "applied and hoped," but "applied as the strongest
  candidate in the pool for roles that match his actual trajectory."
- An application to Imperial/Oxford MSc Mathematics that is a formality,
  because the transcript, recommendation letters, and research exposure
  were being engineered for it three years in advance, not assembled in the
  final two months.
- A person who, on any given evening, is never confused about what the most
  important use of the next hour is — because Athena already resolved that
  question before he sat down.

## What Success Looks Like Day-to-Day

- You open Athena (or it surfaces itself) and within seconds you know: what's
  burning, what's due, what's drifting, and what the single best use of your
  next deep-work block is.
- You are told when a decision you're about to make (or a pattern you're
  falling into) is quietly incompatible with your stated goals — before the
  damage compounds, not after.
- Your 8 PM–midnight deep work window is treated as the most valuable and
  most protected resource in the entire system — nothing trivial is ever
  allowed to occupy it.
- Decision fatigue around "what now?" approaches zero. The system absorbs
  that cognitive tax so you don't have to pay it, semester after semester,
  even as your timetable and priorities change underneath it.

## What Athena Explicitly Is Not

- Not a passive log of events (Calendar).
- Not a flat list of unranked obligations (To-Do apps).
- Not a scheduling optimizer that treats all tasks as equally worthy of a
  slot (Motion AI, Notion databases).
- Not a chatbot you have to interrogate to get value from. Athena pushes;
  you don't have to pull.

Athena's success metric is not "tasks completed." It's **trajectory**:
CGPA slope, skill-acquisition rate, opportunity capture rate, and distance
to the Imperial/Oxford + Quant outcome — measured relentlessly, reported
honestly, and acted on before you'd have noticed the drift yourself.
