# Analytics

### Purpose
A **gentle mirror**, not a scoreboard. This screen exists to reassure and inform — never to judge, shame, or gamify. If a metric could make the user feel bad about themselves, it does not appear here.

---

## Layout

### Weekly Reflection card (top, default view)
- One soft wave-line chart showing the Load Indicator trend (Light/Steady/Full) over the past 4 weeks — no axis numbers, just shape. The takeaway should be visible instantly: "this month has been calmer/heavier than last."
- Directly underneath: one auto-generated, single-sentence, neutral observation (e.g., "Wednesdays tend to be your fullest day") — never phrased as advice or criticism, always phrased as a quiet fact the user can act on or ignore.

### Balance Overview
- A soft stacked color bar (same visual language as Weekly Planner's Balance Bar) averaged over the semester so far — Classes / Study / Work / Personal / Free — pure proportion, no numbers unless tapped.

### Completion, reframed
- Instead of a "completion rate %" or streak counter, show a soft **cluster of dots** — one per day, filled or unfilled — representing "showed up" days, not "perfect" days. A day counts as filled if the user engaged at all, regardless of how much got done. This deliberately removes all-or-nothing thinking from the metric.

### Course Health strip
- One small ring per course (matches Dashboard course tabs) showing a soft blend of "on track / a bit behind / needs attention" — 3 states only, muted colors, never red for "behind," just a slightly cooler tone.

---

## What is deliberately absent
- No leaderboard, no comparison to other users, no productivity score.
- No red/green pass-fail language anywhere.
- No day-by-day guilt trail of missed items — Analytics only shows *patterns*, never individual failures.
- No push notifications driven by this screen ("you're falling behind!") — Analytics is pull-only, viewed when the user chooses to look.

---

## Interaction
- Entirely read-only, swipe between "This Month / This Semester" — two zoom levels only, no custom date ranges (removes decision paralysis).
- Tapping the single-sentence observation can open the relevant view (e.g., tapping "Wednesdays are full" jumps to Weekly Planner) — insight leads directly to action, never just data for data's sake.

---

## Inspiration notes
- **Apple Health/Screen Time**: trend-first, judgment-free framing of personal data.
- **Notion**: soft card-based summary blocks.
- **Linear**: clean, minimal status rings reused consistently from Dashboard/Career View.
- **Arc**: restrained, almost invisible-until-needed presentation — Analytics is one tab away, never front-and-center.
